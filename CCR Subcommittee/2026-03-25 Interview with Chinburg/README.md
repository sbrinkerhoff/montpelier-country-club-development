Chinburg Properties presented a village-center concept for the Country Club Road site proposing roughly 310 units (250 apartments, 60 for-sale homes) in two phases, focused on the lower portion of the site. The most critical discussion points centered on the tension between housing density and preserving the existing recreation fields — which the team admitted they undervalued in their initial proposal — and the community's strong preference for more homeownership units over rentals to attract families and support school enrollment. The team demonstrated significant flexibility throughout, offering to split the oversized 144-unit building, convert smaller apartment buildings into townhouses, and rework the site plan entirely to accommodate the soccer fields and Turtle Island day care. Their vertically integrated construction model (owning both the development and building companies) explains their notably lower per-square-foot costs, and their 40-year track record across a dozen brownfield sites, 2,000+ managed units, and consistent Energy Star certification lends credibility to their execution capability.

The financing discussion revealed that Chinburg is open to carrying the CHIP borrowing risk if they participate in setting the tax increment and deem it reasonable — a meaningful signal given that no one has yet used the CHIP program. Key open items include the incomplete brownfield remediation picture (pesticide contamination in the top two feet, no corrective action plan yet), the need for a validated traffic study for a single-access-point design, and the fact that neither the CEO nor the project lead has visited the site. Public comment from Steve Whitaker raised serious procedural concerns — the consulting contract's undisclosed 3% contingent fee, active litigation in superior court and the Vermont Supreme Court challenging the process, incomplete environmental assessments, and federal compliance challenges filed with HUD — all of which represent real risk to the project timeline regardless of which developer is selected.
